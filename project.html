AI Class Project

RESECON 490AI – Artificial Intelligence in Economics

Project Overview: U.S. Sports Sponsorship Industry

For this project I used AI tools to analyze the U.S. sports sponsorship industry. The report tracks how sports sponsorship revenue in the United States has grown from about $12.1 billion in 2014 to nearly $20 billion in 2024, how COVID-19 caused an estimated $14 billion loss in 2020, and how the market quickly rebounded. Globally, sponsorship is expected to reach around $115 billion by 2025 and $160 billion by 2030, with the U.S. still holding roughly one-third of total share.

A major theme is that sports sponsorship is overtaking ticket sales as the second-largest revenue stream in sports. Growth is driven by assets such as jersey patches, on-field and digital signage, and new sponsor categories like tech, betting, and finance. The project highlights key players (Nike, Adidas, Coca-Cola, Microsoft, major financial institutions) and properties like the NFL, which generated roughly $2.35 billion in sponsorship revenue in 2022–23. I also look at the rapid rise of college Name, Image, and Likeness (NIL), where deals now make up a $1.67 billion market and turn college athletes into important sponsorship partners.

Industry Stage: Growth with Emerging Risks

Using the evidence from my research, I classify the U.S. sports sponsorship industry as being in a strong growth stage. Team sponsorship revenue has climbed to around $8 billion in 2024, and average per-team sponsorship revenue in major leagues is about $51 million. New events such as the 2026 FIFA World Cup and the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics, plus momentum in women’s sports, create additional upside.

At the same time, I discuss risks that could slow the pace of growth. These include potential saturation in top leagues, economic headwinds, and uncertainty around NIL regulations. The project argues that rights-holders and brands will need better data and more strategic targeting to sustain long-run growth in sports sponsorship rather than just adding more logos in more places.

Data Landscape & Role of AI

A key part of the project is mapping the data landscape for sports sponsorship and showing how AI could help make sense of it. I summarize major data sources, including:

  • Nielsen Sports for exposure tracking and sponsorship valuation across thousands of properties.
  • SponsorUnited for detailed deal-level data by brand, property, asset type, and market.
  • Opendorse and On3 for NIL deals, compensation estimates, and athlete–brand relationships.
  • Kaggle and Forbes data sets on athlete earnings and endorsement income over time.
  • Crunchbase for investment and growth data on sponsors, collectives, and sports marketing firms.
  • Macro data from FRED, BLS, and BEA to connect sponsorship spending to the broader economy.

I then outline ways that AI and analytics could deepen this research: time-series models of sponsorship growth, category treemaps comparing sponsor spend by industry, and network graphs mapping connections between brands, teams, athletes, and markets. In the project, AI mainly supports the research workflow—helping organize sources, draft summaries, and propose visualizations—while I provide the final interpretation and judgment about what the trends mean for the future of the U.S. sports sponsorship market.

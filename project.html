RESECON 490AI – Artificial Intelligence in Economics

Final Project Summary

This project analyzes the U.S. sports sponsorship industry, focusing on how the market has evolved over the past decade and what forces are driving its continued growth. The report examines long-run industry trends, the increasing importance of sponsorship as a core revenue stream, and the expansion of commercial assets such as jersey patches, digital signage, and data-driven brand activations. Particular attention is given to the rapid rise of Name, Image, and Likeness (NIL), which has created a new and more decentralized sponsorship marketplace centered around individual athletes.

Using industry data and secondary research, the project evaluates how brands, teams, and athletes create and capture value within this ecosystem, as well as the risks facing the industry, including market saturation and regulatory uncertainty. AI-supported tools were used primarily to assist with research organization and trend identification, while the analysis itself emphasizes economic reasoning and human judgment. Overall, the project provides a structured view of where the sports sponsorship industry stands today and how it is likely to evolve in the near future.
